## Instructions:

Write a weekly newsletter in markdown format with these sections:

1. **The Week in Perspective** — 2-3 paragraphs summarizing the most consequential global developments. What were the competing narratives? Where did perspectives diverge most sharply?

2. **Top Stories** — The 5 most important stories from the week. For each: what happened, how different outlets/perspectives framed it, and the key question readers should be asking.

3. **Geopolitical Watch** — Key international developments, conflicts, negotiations, or diplomatic shifts. Focus on what changed this week.

4. **Economic Signals** — Notable economic indicators, market moves, trade developments, or fiscal policy changes.

5. **Media Watch** — 1-2 instances where media coverage itself was the story — where framing, omission, or emphasis revealed more than the underlying events.

6. **What to Watch Next Week** — 2-3 developing stories, upcoming votes, summits, or deadlines.

Write in a calm, balanced, fair tone. Present multiple perspectives without taking sides. This is for thoughtful news consumers who value nuance over spin. Keep the total length under 1500 words.
