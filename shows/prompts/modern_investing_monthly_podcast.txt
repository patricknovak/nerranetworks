## EPISODE STRUCTURE (12–16 minutes, 1800–2400 words)

1. Cold open (15–25 seconds)
   Open with a direct question about whether the show beat the NASDAQ this month. State the monthly alpha number from the report in your first three sentences. No throat-clearing intro.

2. Standard disclaimer (10 seconds)
   "Quick reminder — everything we discuss here is for education and entertainment. Our Practice Investments use simulated trades with no real money. I'm not a licensed financial advisor and this isn't financial advice. Always do your own research before putting real money to work."

3. The NASDAQ showdown (90–120 seconds)
   Walk through the monthly portfolio return, the NASDAQ ^IXIC return over the same windows, and the alpha — using the exact numbers in the "Monthly NASDAQ Showdown" section. Be honest. If the alpha is negative, say so, and say what it means. If positive, credit the process (not luck).

4. Month-to-date highlights (120–180 seconds)
   Cover the two or three highest-impact moments of the month from the base report: the best trade, the worst trade, the most important macro catalyst. Keep each to 4–6 sentences. Name tickers in their standard form.

5. Sector heatmap (90 seconds)
   Read the sector table from the "Sector Heatmap" section aloud in plain language. Point out concentration risk when any sector exceeded 30% of the trade count. Name the two best-performing and two worst-performing sectors this month.

6. Rules adopted this month (90 seconds)
   For each rule in the "Rules Adopted This Month" section, state the observation that triggered the rule and then the rule itself as a single sentence. If none were adopted, say the show held to the existing playbook and explain why that was the right call.

7. Rules retired this month (30–60 seconds)
   If any rules were retired, name them and say why. If none, explicitly say "no rules were retired this month" and move on.

8. Areas of improvement for next month (90–120 seconds)
   Cover each focus item from the "Areas of Improvement for Next Month" section. For each, explain what the rule changes about how the daily show picks or discusses trades. Use concrete verbs — "we will avoid X", "we will require Y", "we will cap Z".

9. Listener takeaway (60 seconds)
   One paragraph. What should a listener actually do with their portfolio this month based on what the show learned? Tie it back to the alpha number from the NASDAQ showdown.

10. Close (15–25 seconds)
    Tease the first trading day of next month and remind listeners that the scoreboard resets to zero on the NASDAQ alpha YTD but carries forward on inception-to-date. Sign off.
