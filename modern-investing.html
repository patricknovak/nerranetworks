About Modern Investing Techniques

Modern Investing Techniques teaches real investing strategies using AI analysis, modern tools, and emerging market techniques. Each episode covers market conditions, features a Strategy Spotlight deep-dive, presents an AI-selected Practice Investment of the Day (simulated), and reviews yesterday's trade performance — wins and losses, with honest analysis.

The goal: help everyday investors consistently outperform index fund returns using tools that used to be reserved for hedge funds and institutional traders.

Hosted by Patrick in Vancouver. Canadian-first perspective with global market awareness.

Key Concepts

What is a TFSA?
A Tax-Free Savings Account lets Canadian residents invest and grow money completely tax-free. Contributions aren't tax-deductible, but all investment gains — dividends, capital gains, interest — are never taxed, even when withdrawn. The 2026 contribution limit is announced annually by CRA. It's one of the most powerful wealth-building tools available to Canadians.
What is the Practice Investment of the Day?
Each episode, AI analysis selects one specific investment for a simulated same-day trade — buy at market open, sell at market close. This is purely educational: no real money is involved. We track performance across episodes, discuss wins and losses honestly, and use each trade to teach a specific analytical technique. The goal is to demonstrate how AI-driven analysis works, not to provide stock picks.
What does 'outperform index funds' mean?
Index funds like those tracking the S&P 500 or TSX Composite historically return 7-10% annually. Outperforming means using strategies — momentum, sector rotation, technical analysis, AI screening — to achieve higher returns. It requires more active management and carries more risk. This show teaches the strategies and tools to help you decide if active investing suits your goals.
What is RSI?
The Relative Strength Index is a momentum indicator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a scale of 0-100. Below 30 suggests a stock may be oversold (potential buying opportunity). Above 70 suggests it may be overbought (potential selling signal). It's one of the most commonly used technical analysis tools discussed on the show.
Is this financial advice?
No. Everything on Modern Investing Techniques is for education and entertainment only. The Practice Investment of the Day uses simulated trades with no real money. Patrick is not a licensed financial advisor. Always do your own research and consult a qualified financial advisor before making investment decisions.
