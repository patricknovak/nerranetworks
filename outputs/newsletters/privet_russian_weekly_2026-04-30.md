### 2. Grammar Patterns

This week we focused on **simple weather expressions**. Russian often uses short adverbs instead of full sentences:

- **Холодно** = It’s cold  
- **Тепло** = It’s warm  
- **Идёт дождь** = It’s raining (literally “rain is going”)

**Examples using this week’s words:**
- Сегодня **тепло**, но сильный **ветер**.
- Зимой очень **холодно** и много **снега**.
- В **лесу** живёт **медведь**, но он не любит **гроза**.

Don’t worry about perfect grammar yet — just practicing these patterns out loud is fantastic progress!

### 3. Culture Corner Recap

We discovered that Russians talk about **погода** *constantly* because the weather can change dramatically in one day (classic “four seasons in one afternoon” energy). We also learned that the **медведь** (bear) isn’t just an animal in Russia — it’s a national symbol that appears in fairy tales, art, and even politics. The **лес** (forest) is a magical place in Russian culture, full of talking animals and adventures. You’re now equipped to discuss both the sky *and* the enchanted woods!

### 4. Practice Challenge

**“Weather + Animal Safari”** (perfect for beginners):

1. Look out your window and say (out loud!) at least three weather sentences in Russian.  
   *Example: “Сегодня **солнце**, но есть **облако**. Не **холодно**.”*

2. Draw or find pictures of three animals you like. Label them in Russian (**собака**, **кошка**, **медведь**, etc.).

3. Bonus Molodets move: Combine them! “Я люблю **собака**. **Медведь** живёт в **лес**.”

Takes only 10 minutes but gives your brain huge wins. You’ve got this!
