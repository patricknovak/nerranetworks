### 2. Grammar Patterns

This week we practiced **describing the world around us** with simple, ready-to-use phrases:

- **Сегодня + adjective**: Сегодня тепло. / Сегодня холодно.
- **Идёт** for weather in progress: Идёт дождь. (It’s raining.)
- Location with **в + prepositional case**: В лесу живёт медведь. (A bear lives in the forest.)
- Simple subject + verb: Солнце светит. Птица летает. Собака бегает.

These patterns let you start speaking in full sentences right away — exactly what beginners need!

### 3. Culture Corner Recap

We learned that Russians talk about **погода** constantly (it’s a national conversation starter). We also discovered that the Russian word for bear — **медведь** — literally comes from “мёд” (honey) + “ведать” (to know). In other words, “the one who knows where the honey is!” 

Russia’s vast forests (леса) are famous for brown bears, wolves, and foxes, so the animal episode felt very Russian. Even the weather words felt cozy — Russians have a special respect for cold winters and the first warm days of spring.
