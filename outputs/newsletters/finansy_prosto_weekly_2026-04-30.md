### Top Stories

**1. CRA Scams Are Everywhere — Here’s How to Stay Safe**  
CRA will never send you an email with a link asking for personal information, SIN, or payment. Real refunds and notices don’t work that way. With the April 30 deadline looming, scammers are working overtime. The safest approach: type canada.ca manually, use Wealthsimple Tax if you need something simple and fast, and never click suspicious links. In a city where rent, ICBC, and BC Hydro already devour budgets, losing even $500 to fraud hurts.

**2. Unused RRSP Room Can Protect GIS Benefits**  
Many listeners discovered their parents had significant unused RRSP contribution room. By making a contribution and applying the deduction against investment income (interest, dividends, capital gains), you can reduce taxable income enough to preserve the Guaranteed Income Supplement. For seniors on fixed incomes in the Lower Mainland, every $50–$200 monthly loss in GIS is deeply felt. This strategy is a legitimate, CRA-approved way to fight back.

**3. Bank of Canada Holds Rate at 2¼%**  
No change for the fourth consecutive announcement. Variable-rate mortgage holders and people renewing this year get temporary stability. However, the Bank highlighted rising global uncertainty and warned inflation may run hotter than expected. For families in Richmond, Coquitlam, or Surrey, this means mortgage payments probably won’t jump in the next few months — valuable breathing room while you finish taxes and rebuild emergency funds.

**4. Canada Launches “Canada Strong Fund” Sovereign Wealth Fund**  
The government announced its own sovereign wealth fund, though details on funding sources and governance are still thin. In a country where resource revenue and long-term planning matter, this could eventually support pensions, family benefits, and cost-of-living programs. For those of us watching housing prices and public services in Vancouver, anything that strengthens the nation’s “family savings account” deserves attention.

**5. What to Actually Do With Your Tax Refund**  
Instead of spending it, the episodes strongly suggested using refunds to contribute to TFSA/RRSP, top up emergency funds, or pay down high-interest debt. With interest rates stable but uncertainty rising, liquidity and tax-efficient investing remain the smartest moves for women managing family budgets in expensive Canadian cities.
