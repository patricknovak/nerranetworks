Cosmic Spotlight

The idea that space weather might be scrambling alien radio signals is a fresh take on the Fermi paradox. Researchers modeled how interstellar plasma could broaden narrowband signals into undetectable smears, based on data from SETI searches and plasma physics. This opens doors to redesigning detectors for broader signals, potentially boosting our chances of finding cosmic neighbors. What if we adjusted our tech—could we finally hear from out there?

Today's stories blend cosmic mysteries with practical space tech, reminding us how the universe keeps surprising. Keep looking up!
