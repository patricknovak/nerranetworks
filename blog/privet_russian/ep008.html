Привет, Русский! — Episode Plan

Date: March 30, 2026

Art (Искусство)

Vocabulary List (10 words/phrases):

  • Russian (Cyrillic): искусство

Transliteration: ees-KOOSST-voh

English: art

Example sentence: Я люблю искусство.

Example translation: I love art.

Memory hook: Sounds a bit like “is cost-vo” — imagine asking “what is the cost of this beautiful art?”

  • Russian (Cyrillic): картина

Transliteration: kar-TEE-nah

English: painting / picture

Example sentence: Это красивая картина.

Example translation: This is a beautiful painting.

Memory hook: Sounds like “cartoon” but it’s a real painting!

  • Russian (Cyrillic): музей

Transliteration: moo-ZEY

English: museum

Example sentence: Мы идём в музей.

Example translation: We are going to the museum.

Memory hook: Almost exactly the same as English “museum” — just with a Russian accent!

  • Russian (Cyrillic): красть / украли

Transliteration: krast’ / oo-KRA-lee

English: to steal / they stole

Example sentence: Воры украли картину.

Example translation: Thieves stole the painting.

Memory hook: “Ukrali” sounds like “ooh, crawl away!” — the thieves crawled away with the painting.

  • Russian (Cyrillic): вор

Transliteration: vor

English: thief

Example sentence: Вор взял картину.

Example translation: The thief took the painting.

Memory hook: Sounds like “war” — but these “wars” are sneaky art thieves!

  • Russian (Cyrillic): миллион

Transliteration: mee-lee-ON

English: million

Example sentence: Картина стоит миллион.

Example translation: The painting costs a million.

Memory hook: Almost identical to English “million”!

  • Russian (Cyrillic): красивый

Transliteration: kra-SEE-vyy

English: beautiful

Example sentence: Это очень красивый рисунок.

Example translation: This is a very beautiful drawing.

Memory hook: “Krasivyy” — think “crazy beautiful!”

  • Russian (Cyrillic): рисовать

Transliteration: ree-so-VAT’

English: to draw / to paint

Example sentence: Я люблю рисовать.

Example translation: I love to draw.

Memory hook: Sounds like “re-so-vat” — imagine you “re-so” (redo) your drawing until it’s perfect.

  • Russian (Cyrillic): художник

Transliteration: hoo-DOZH-neek

English: artist

Example sentence: Ренуар был знаменитым художником.

Example translation: Renoir was a famous artist.

Memory hook: Sounds like “who’s the genius?” — the artist is the genius!

  • Russian (Cyrillic): маска

Transliteration: MAS-kah

English: mask

Example sentence: Воры были в масках.

Example translation: The thieves were wearing masks.

Memory hook: Exactly the same as English “mask”!

Grammar Spotlight:

The Past Tense of Verbs (simple past)

In Russian, most verbs form the past tense by replacing the infinitive ending with -л (for masculine), -ла (feminine), -ло (neuter), or -ли (plural or polite “you”).

  • Украли (they stole) ← красть
  • Рисовал (he drew/painted) ← рисовать
  • Был (he was) ← быть

Compare to English: Russian past tense is actually easier — no complicated “was/were/had” rules. Just add -л/-ла/-ли and you’re done! We will practice this more in future episodes.

Word Origins — Deep Dive: картина

Did you know that the Russian word картина and the English word cartoon are actually cousins? They both come from the same Italian root!

This word started in Italian as cartone — a large, strong sheet of paper used for sketches. It travelled through French into both English and Russian around the 18th–19th centuries. In English it became “cartoon” (first meaning a sketch, later funny drawings). In Russian it became картина, which kept the original meaning of “painting” or “picture.”

But be careful — this is a fun false friend moment! The Russian word for “cartoon” (like animated film) is actually мультфильм (moolt-FEELM), not картина!

Next time you watch a cartoon or see a beautiful painting, remember: картина and “cartoon” are long-lost relatives. That connection is your secret shortcut to remembering the word forever.

Cultural Corner:

Russians have a deep love for art and museums. The Tretyakov Gallery in Moscow and the Hermitage in Saint Petersburg are world-famous and visited by millions every year. Many Russian children study art in school, and painting is considered a wonderful hobby that develops patience and creativity. Even today, Russians say “Искусство спасёт мир” — “Art will save the world.”

Practice Challenge:

“My Museum” Game (great for kids and adults!)

Draw or describe your own imaginary museum at home. Use at least 6 words from today’s list. Example:

“В моём музее есть красивая картина. Художник рисовал её очень долго. Она стоит миллион!”

Then say it out loud and record yourself. Parents — ask your child to repeat the sentences after you slowly. Repeat after me:

Кра-си-ва-я кар-ти-на… (pause) Красивая картина!
