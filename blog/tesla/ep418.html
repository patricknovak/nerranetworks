Tesla First Principles

🧠 Tesla First Principles - Cutting Through the Noise

Taking a step back from today's headlines, let's apply first principles thinking to whether Tesla's Supercharger network can generate enough margin and strategic value to justify continued heavy investment, or will it become a drag.

The Surprising Truth: The network is one of the few physical assets Tesla owns that directly touches almost every owner on a regular basis, yet many assume it must be either a loss leader or a massive profit centre when the reality sits somewhere in between.

The Fundamental Question: What combination of utilization, pricing power and strategic importance actually makes the Supercharger business worthwhile long term?

The Data Says: Real-world charging sessions provide recurring revenue from both Tesla and non-Tesla vehicles while the physical locations create data on travel patterns that feed into routing and energy products, creating a tighter feedback loop than pure software can achieve.

The Tesla Approach: Tesla would treat the chargers like any other engineering problem — measure actual duty cycles and energy throughput at each site, iterate on placement and power levels based on observed demand, and integrate them tightly with vehicle software and the Megapack business rather than running them as a standalone operation.

The Bottom Line: If the network can deliver both direct margin and irreplaceable ecosystem lock-in, it becomes a durable advantage; if it cannot scale efficiently as more vehicles come online it risks turning into an expensive maintenance item that dilutes focus.
