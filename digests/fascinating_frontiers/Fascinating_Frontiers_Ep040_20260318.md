### Cosmic Spotlight
The detection of all five DNA nucleobases in material from asteroid Ryugu is one of the most exciting findings this week. Both Ryugu and Bennu samples now show adenine, guanine, cytosine, thymine, and uracil — the complete set of components needed for DNA and RNA. This tells us that the basic chemistry for life was likely present in the early solar system and could have been delivered to Earth by asteroids and comets billions of years ago.  

What makes this especially compelling is that these molecules survived the violent processes of asteroid formation and the journey back to Earth.  

If similar chemistry turns up on other small bodies, how much does that raise our confidence that life’s ingredients were widespread throughout the solar system?
