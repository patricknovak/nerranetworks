### Cosmic Spotlight
Let’s zoom in on the candidate millisecond pulsar near the Milky Way’s galactic center (#3). Discovered by Breakthrough Listen and Columbia University researchers, this pulsar—if confirmed—sits close to our galaxy’s supermassive black hole, creating a natural laboratory to test Einstein’s General Relativity. Pulsars act like cosmic clocks, emitting signals with astonishing regularity, and the black hole’s immense gravity should distort these signals in ways Einstein predicted. This could provide the most precise evidence yet of how spacetime bends under extreme forces. The next step is confirmation through further observations, potentially unlocking deeper secrets of gravity’s behavior.
