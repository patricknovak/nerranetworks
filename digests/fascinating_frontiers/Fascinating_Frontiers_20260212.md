By analyzing these rocks with onboard instruments like the drill and spectrometers, scientists are piecing together evidence of past environmental conditions that could have supported microbial life. This ongoing exploration not only refines our map of Mars' geological evolution but also informs future human missions by identifying resource-rich areas.
