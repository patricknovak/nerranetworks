### Cosmic Spotlight
Let’s zoom in on the James Webb Space Telescope’s groundbreaking 3D mapping of Uranus’s auroras (#4). For the first time, scientists have peered into the vertical structure of this ice giant’s upper atmosphere, observing temperature shifts and charged particles dancing high above its clouds. Using JWST’s NIRSpec instrument, the team captured the planet’s faint molecular glow over nearly a full rotation, unveiling how energy flows in these mysterious layers. This isn’t just about Uranus—it’s a window into understanding giant exoplanets beyond our solar system, where similar atmospheric dynamics might hide.
