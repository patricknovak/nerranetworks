### Cosmic Spotlight
Let’s dive deeper into #4: **Enceladus’ Electromagnetic Powerhouse**. Saturn’s icy moon Enceladus, just 500 km wide, wields an astonishing influence, generating electromagnetic waves called Alfvén wings that stretch over half a million kilometers through Saturn’s magnetosphere. Discovered through 13 years of Cassini spacecraft data, these waves form a lattice-like structure, bouncing between Saturn’s ionosphere and a plasma torus around Enceladus’ orbit. This tiny moon’s outsized impact challenges our assumptions about planetary interactions, suggesting that even small bodies can shape cosmic environments in profound ways. What’s next could involve studying similar effects on Jupiter’s moons or even exoplanetary systems, potentially uncovering hidden dynamics across the universe.
