### Top 15 Space & Astronomy Stories
1. **NASA Tests Solar Oxygen Extraction on Moon: 27 February 2026 • NASA**
   NASA's Carbothermal Reduction Demonstration successfully integrated a solar concentrator with mirrors and software to produce carbon monoxide from simulated lunar regolith. This advances in-situ resource utilization, enabling future lunar bases to generate oxygen from local materials for life support and fuel.
   Source: https://www.nasa.gov/image-detail/carbothermal-reduction-demonstration-card-project-testing-2/

2. **NASA Cancels Artemis 3 Moon Landing: 27 February 2026 • Space.com**
   NASA is reworking its Artemis missions, scrapping the planned 2028 astronaut moon landing due to unrealistic timelines. The shift focuses on foundational steps to ensure sustainable lunar exploration.
   Source: https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/nasa-cancels-artemis-3-astronaut-moon-landing-this-is-just-not-the-right-pathway-forward

3. **Ancient Impact Evidence Found in Brazil: 27 February 2026 • Universe Today**
   Researchers discovered a tektite field in Brazil indicating a major asteroid impact about 6 million years ago. This finding reveals details of Earth's geological history and the spread of impact debris across multiple provinces.
   Source: https://www.universetoday.com/articles/6-million-years-ago-something-slammed-into-modern-day-brazil

4. **NASA Overhauls Artemis Moon Program: 27 February 2026 • Spaceflight Now**
   New NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman announced changes to Artemis, including a preparatory mission before any 2028 landing. The overhaul aims to build realistic groundwork for enduring human presence on the Moon.
   Source: https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/02/27/nasa-announces-major-overhaul-of-artemis-moon-program/

5. **JWST Probes Uranus Ionosphere Structure: 27 February 2026 • Universe Today**
   The James Webb Space Telescope used its NIRSpec instrument to map Uranus's ionosphere, detecting auroral bands and magnetic field variations. These observations provide insights into the planet's extreme tilt and atmospheric dynamics.
   Source: https://www.universetoday.com/articles/jwst-digs-into-the-uranian-ionosphere

6. **Upcoming Total Lunar Eclipse Details: 27 February 2026 • Space.com**
   The March 3 total lunar eclipse will be the last until New Year's Eve 2028, followed by a rare trifecta in 2028-2029. This event offers a chance to observe Earth's shadow fully obscuring the Moon.
   Source: https://www.space.com/stargazing/lunar-eclipses/the-total-lunar-eclipse-on-march-3-will-be-the-last-until-new-years-eve-2028

7. **Why Moon Turns Red in Eclipses: 27 February 2026 • Astronomy Magazine**
   Earth's atmosphere bends red sunlight into its shadow during lunar eclipses, scattering blue light and giving the Moon an orange hue. This phenomenon explains the "blood moon" appearance and ties to atmospheric optics.
   Source: https://www.astronomy.com/science/why-does-the-moon-turn-red-during-a-lunar-eclipse-2/

8. **NASA Advances Experimental Flight Research: 27 February 2026 • NASA**
   NASA's Armstrong Flight Research Center in California is pioneering high-speed, autonomous, and electrified aircraft technologies. These efforts support broader aeronautics goals, from breaking sound barriers to future aviation innovations.
   Source: https://www.nasa.gov/centers-and-facilities/armstrong/nasas-home-for-experimental-flight-advances-aeronautics-mission/

9. **NASA Adds Mission to Artemis Program: 27 February 2026 • NASA**
   NASA is increasing Artemis mission cadence with a new 2027 flight to standardize vehicles and prepare for surface operations. This update aims to achieve sustainable lunar exploration and astronaut returns.
   Source: https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-adds-mission-to-artemis-lunar-program-updates-architecture/

10. **Curiosity Finds Water Clues in Mars Rocks: 27 February 2026 • Space.com**
   NASA's Curiosity rover imaged web-like features on Mars, likely carved by ancient groundwater. These rocky "spiderwebs" offer evidence of the planet's watery past and potential habitability.
   Source: https://www.space.com/space-exploration/mars-rovers/curiosity-rover-finds-clues-to-mars-watery-past-in-rocky-spiderwebs

11. **Birth of Solar Radio Astronomy: 27 February 2026 • Astronomy Magazine**
   On February 27, 1942, physicist James Stanley Hey identified solar interference on radar as the start of solar radio astronomy. This discovery opened new ways to study the Sun's emissions and space weather.
   Source: https://www.astronomy.com/today-in-the-history-of-astronomy/feb-27-1942-the-birth-of-solar-radio-astronomy/
