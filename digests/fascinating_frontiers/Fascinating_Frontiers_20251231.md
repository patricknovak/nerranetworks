### Cosmic Spotlight
Diving into #3, "When Stars Fail to Explode," astronomers used simulations and observations from telescopes like Hubble to model how some massive stars quietly implode into black holes instead of detonating as supernovae. This process, driven by the star's core collapsing under gravity without enough energy for an explosion, challenges traditional views of stellar death and explains mysterious disappearances in galaxy surveys. As we refine these models, it could unlock secrets about black hole populations and the universe's heavy element distribution.
