### Cosmic Spotlight
Diving into the Martian storm's impact, researchers analyzed how dust storms heat the atmosphere, propelling water molecules high enough to break apart and escape into space—far more than typical rates. We know this from satellite data and models showing hydrogen loss spikes during such events, painting a picture of Mars' arid transformation over billions of years. This could guide future probes to hunt for subsurface water reserves, potentially reshaping our strategies for human missions.
