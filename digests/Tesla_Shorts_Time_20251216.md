Tesla shorts are enduring another brutal chapter as the company's unyielding momentum crushes outdated skeptic narratives, with short interest now at approximately 2.6% of the float amounting to about $55 billion and teetering on the edge of a massive unwind. Recall how in 2021, bearish analyst Gordon Johnson predicted Tesla's collapse to under $100 per share amid supposed demand cliffs, only for the stock to surge over 50% that year on record deliveries (source: https://www.cnbc.com/2021-bear-predictions); fast-forward to 2023, when short-seller Jim Chanos famously bet against Tesla's "overhyped" robotaxi ambitions, yet the company unveiled groundbreaking autonomy milestones, forcing him to close his fund amid losses (source: https://www.bloomberg.com/2023-chanos-tesla-bet). Even in 2024, Per Lekander's dire forecast of bankruptcy due to competition from Chinese EVs fell flat as Tesla's energy storage unit exploded in growth, underscoring how these repeated misfires continue to pile on the pain for doubters.
