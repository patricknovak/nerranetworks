### Strategy Spotlight
**Fuel-Cost Relief Rotation Strategy**

This strategy involves identifying companies or sectors that benefit from government fuel-price subsidies or excise-tax removals, then rotating capital toward those names to capture margin expansion as input costs decline. Today's news of Germany's €1.6 billion ($1.9 billion) fuel-price relief package and the Philippines' removal of kerosene and LPG taxes to temper food inflation provides a textbook catalyst: lower energy costs flow directly to consumer-facing and logistics-heavy businesses, improving free-cash-flow forecasts.

Implementation is straightforward on platforms like Questrade or Wealthsimple. Screen for companies with high fuel or energy exposure (look at SG&A or cost-of-goods-sold breakdowns in recent 10-K/annual reports), then compare forward P/E ratios before and after the relief announcement. Enter on the first 2–3 days of confirmed policy language, size positions to 3–5% of portfolio, and set trailing stops at 5–7% below entry to protect gains.

The approach has historically worked best in the first 30–60 days after relief packages are announced, when analysts revise margin estimates upward but before the relief is fully priced in. Risks include policy reversal or offsetting spikes in other commodities. Always cross-reference volume — relief-driven moves with above-average 20-day volume carry higher probability.

### Investor Education: Tax-Loss Harvesting Clock Management
Imagine you sold 200 shares of a Canadian mining stock in your TFSA on March 15 after it dropped 12%, booking the loss to offset capital gains elsewhere in your non-registered account. The order filled at $18.40, but because you repurchased identical shares in your RRSP just 18 days later, the CRA applied the superficial loss rule and denied the entire $480 tax benefit.

The superficial loss rule disallows a capital loss if you (or an affiliated person) acquire an identical property 30 days before or after the sale. This 61-day window is unforgiving: even buying in a different account or through a spouse can trigger it. In 2025–2026, with the capital-gains inclusion rate at 66.67% for gains over $250,000, a denied $10,000 loss costs an investor roughly $2,222 in immediate tax deferral.

**Pro tip:** Professionals always maintain a “substitute basket” of highly correlated but non-identical securities (different ticker, same sector beta) to stay invested while the 30-day clock runs. Check the ACB (adjusted cost base) tracker in your brokerage portal before every sale.

The biggest mistake is assuming account silos protect you. Instead, always run a 61-day forward calendar check and document substitute securities before harvesting losses. This single mechanic prevents more unexpected tax bills than any other retail error.
