### Cool Stuff & Try This
**[AI Hunts Down Hidden Bugs in Your Browser]: The Decoder**  
Anthropic's Claude AI (a large language model, which is basically a super-advanced text predictor trained on tons of data) discovered over 100 security vulnerabilities in Firefox, the popular web browser, including ones missed by years of human testing. It's cool because it shows how AI can make the internet safer by spotting weak spots faster than people, which matters for everyday stuff like safe online gaming or social media scrolling. Students curious about tech careers should check this out – it highlights AI's role in cybersecurity. Go to Claude.ai (free to use with an account), paste a simple code snippet from a tutorial site, and ask "Find any bugs in this" to see it analyze like a pro.  
Source: https://the-decoder.com/anthropics-claude-ai-uncovers-over-100-security-vulnerabilities-in-firefox/

**[Catching AI "Hallucinations" with Spilled Energy]: The Decoder**  
Researchers found that when large language models hallucinate (make up false info because they guess wrong instead of knowing facts), they leave behind measurable "spilled energy" in their internal math calculations, and there's now a simple way to detect it without extra training. This is exciting because it helps make AI more reliable for things like homework help or fact-checking social media claims, reducing those annoying made-up answers. Anyone starting with AI should try this to build trust in tools. Head to ChatGPT (free at chat.openai.com) and ask something tricky like "Tell me about the ancient city of Atlantica" – watch for invented details, then cross-check with Google to spot the hallucination in action.  
Source: https://the-decoder.com/when-language-models-hallucinate-they-leave-spilled-energy-in-their-own-math/

This is so cool for aspiring creators, as it brings pro-level video AI closer to everyday use in games or TikTok edits!

**[Smart AI for Math and Screens]**  
Microsoft launched Phi-4-Reasoning-Vision-15B, a compact multimodal model (one that handles both text and images) that's great at math, science, and understanding app interfaces like phone screens. It's awesome for students, as it could power tools that explain homework or analyze game UIs without needing massive computers.
