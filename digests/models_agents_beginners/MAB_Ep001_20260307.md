### The Big Story
An AI company called Anthropic refused a $200 million deal with the U.S. Pentagon because they couldn't agree on rules for using AI in military stuff like autonomous weapons or mass surveillance. Think of it like a superhero turning down a shady job offer to stay true to their values – Anthropic didn't want their tech twisted for harmful purposes, so the Pentagon went with OpenAI instead, causing a big backlash with tons of people uninstalling ChatGPT. This is a big deal because it shows AI isn't neutral; companies have to decide how much control governments get over powerful tools that could affect wars or privacy. For you, it highlights why ethics matter in tech – imagine if AI in your favorite games or social apps started spying on you without checks. As a teen or student, this could inspire you to think about careers in AI where you focus on making it fair and safe. It might make everyday AI chats feel more trustworthy knowing some creators prioritize good over money. Right now, chat with an AI like ChatGPT about "AI ethics in the military" to explore these ideas yourself – it's free and eye-opening!

### Cool Stuff & Try This
**[AI Gets Graded on App-Making Skills: MarkTechPost]**
Google released something called Android Bench, which is basically a report card and testing system to see how well large language models (LLMs – super-smart AIs trained on tons of text to chat, write, and even code) handle tasks for building Android apps, like those on your phone. It's cool because it pushes AI to get better at helping create games or social apps you love, making app development faster and more accessible for beginners dreaming of tech careers. If you're into gaming or want to make your own app someday, this means AI could soon be your free tutor for stuff like coding a simple game. Go to chat.google.com (Google's Gemini chat) and ask it to "explain how to make a basic Android app idea for a homework tracker" – no coding needed, just let the AI guide you with steps!
Source: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/06/google-ai-releases-android-bench-an-evaluation-framework-and-leaderboard-for-llms-in-android-development/

**[OpenAI's New Security Sidekick: OpenAI News]**
OpenAI launched Codex Security, an AI tool that acts like a smart detective for software projects, spotting and fixing tricky security weak spots (like bugs that could let hackers in) by understanding the whole project's context. It's exciting because it could make apps and games safer from crashes or cheats, which matters for stuff you use daily like social media or online homework tools. Students or curious teens into tech safety should check this out – it shows how AI is becoming a guardian for the digital world. Head to chat.openai.com and ask ChatGPT to "simulate finding a bug in a simple game app description" to get a feel for how these tools think – it's a fun, free way to experiment!
Source: https://openai.com/index/codex-security-now-in-research-preview
