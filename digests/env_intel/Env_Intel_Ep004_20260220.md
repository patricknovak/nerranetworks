### Lead Story
The BC Ministry of Energy and Climate Solutions announced an expansion of the CleanBC Better Homes funding program, providing up to $800,000 per project for energy retrofits in social and non-profit housing under EMA s. 47 emission reduction mandates. Previously, funding capped at $500,000 with narrower eligibility focused on single-family dwellings; the update broadens to multi-unit non-profits and emphasizes GHG reductions aligned with BC's 2030 targets. Changes include streamlined applications for upgrades like heat pumps and insulation, reducing Scope 1 and 2 emissions by up to 40% per site based on prior program data. For practitioners, this means integrating energy assessments into Phase III remediation plans for contaminated housing sites, potentially offsetting costs via grants during CSR certificate of compliance pursuits. Watch for program guidelines updates in Q2 2026, with initial applications due by March 31, 2026. Consultation on further EMA amendments related to building emissions is expected by April 2026.

### Regulatory & Policy Watch
**Paris Court Holds Historic Climate Trial in Case Against TotalEnergies: Inside Climate News**  
A Paris Judicial Court initiated a civil trial compelling TotalEnergies to align oil and gas production with the Paris Agreement's 1.5°C limit, potentially setting precedents for emitter liability under international accords that influence CEPA 2024 amendments on corporate GHG accountability. For BC professionals, this could affect environmental lawyers handling litigation against major emitters like those in the LNG sector, mirroring EMA s. 91 enforcement for climate damages. Monitor for trial outcomes by Q3 2026, with potential cross-border implications for federal Impact Assessment Act reviews of energy projects.  
Source: https://insideclimatenews.org/news/19022026/paris-totalenergies-climate-trial/
