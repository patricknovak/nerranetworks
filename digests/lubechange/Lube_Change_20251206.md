Did you know that in the 1984-85 season, the Oilers set an NHL record for the most goals scored by a team in a single season with 446, showcasing their unstoppable offensive dynasty? This feat, led by stars like Wayne Gretzky and Jari Kurri, highlighted Edmonton's revolutionary fast-paced style that changed hockey forever. Even today, it remains a benchmark for scoring prowess that few teams have approached.
