**TOPIC SELECTION:** Charging infrastructure scale versus utilization

Taking a step back from today's headlines, let's apply first principles thinking to the real economics of public charging networks...

**The Surprising Truth:** The value of a charging stall isn't just how many you build, it's how often each one is actually used and how much revenue it generates per day.

**The Fundamental Question:** What actually determines whether Tesla's Supercharger network becomes a durable competitive advantage or just an expensive utility?

**The Data Says:** With legacy automakers like Stellantis now plugging in, the total addressable vehicles for Tesla's stalls has grown significantly while the physical network stays the same, which should lift average utilization rates and improve the return on the capital already spent.

**The Tesla Approach:** Tesla treats the Supercharger network like any other engineering problem: measure real usage data, price dynamically where possible, open the network to non-Tesla vehicles to fill idle stalls, and keep building only where the numbers justify it rather than chasing arbitrary coverage targets.

**The Bottom Line:** Opening the network to Stellantis, Jeep, Dodge and Ram vehicles turns idle capacity into revenue and strengthens the economic case for further expansion, showing that utilization and business model matter more than raw stall count.
