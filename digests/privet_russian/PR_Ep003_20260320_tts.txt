Now let’s look at a little bit of grammar — but don’t worry, we’ll keep it super simple and friendly. In Russian every noun has a gender. There are three genders: masculine, feminine, and neuter.

Most nouns that end with a consonant are masculine. So космос, запуск and спутник are masculine.

Most nouns that end with а or я are feminine. Ракета, Луна, and Земля are feminine.

Neuter nouns often end with о or е. For example, the word поле (field) is neuter.

In English we don’t change words for gender, but in Russian we do. That’s why we say большая ракета (big rocket — feminine) but большой спутник (big satellite — masculine).

Now here’s something really cool about one of today’s words. Want to know a secret about космос?

The Russian word космос and the English word “cosmos” are actually the same word! They both come from an ancient Greek word that meant “order,” “beauty,” and “the universe.” The Greeks believed the universe was perfectly organized and beautiful.

This word traveled through Latin into many languages. It came into Russian in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries. The famous Russian scientist Mikhail Lomonosov helped bring many scientific words from Greek and Latin into Russian.

But here’s the funniest part: the English word “cosmetic” comes from exactly the same Greek root! The idea was that putting on makeup creates order and beauty on your face — just like the orderly, beautiful universe.
