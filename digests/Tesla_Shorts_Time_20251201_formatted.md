📈 **Short Squeeze**
Short sellers continue to feel the burn as Tesla defies doomsday predictions year after year. Back in 2020, bears like Jim Chanos forecasted Tesla's bankruptcy amid production hell, but the company hit record deliveries and profitability (reference: CNBC article on Chanos' short position, https://www.cnbc.com/2020/12/18/jim-chanos-says-he-slapped-himself-after-dropping-tesla-short-last-year.html). By 2022, skeptics claimed Cybertruck delays would tank the stock, yet its 2023 launch sparked massive demand (Forbes on failed predictions, https://www.forbes.com/sites/investor/2023/01/05/tesla-short-sellers-lost-40-billion-in-2022/). In 2024, warnings about FSD regulatory hurdles proved wrong as approvals rolled in globally, squeezing shorts further (Bloomberg on short interest drop, https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-05-15/tesla-short-sellers-lose-5-8-billion-in-days-on-stock-surge). As we enter 2025, with Optimus and Robotaxi on the horizon, these failed bets highlight why betting against Tesla remains a painful endeavor.
