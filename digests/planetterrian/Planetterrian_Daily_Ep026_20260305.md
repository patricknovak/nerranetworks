### Top 15 Science & Health Discoveries
1. **Darwin’s jars analyzed via laser without opening** : 05 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Scientists used laser techniques to examine the chemical makeup of preservation fluids in Charles Darwin’s 200-year-old Galápagos specimen jars without opening them. This method could safeguard millions of museum specimens by revealing historical preservation details non-invasively.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260303145720.htm

2. **Amazon insects vulnerable to heat stress** : 05 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   A study of over 2,000 insect species showed many in tropical lowlands lack heat tolerance flexibility, risking ecosystem disruption from rising temperatures. This highlights potential biodiversity losses in high-diversity areas, affecting pollination and decomposition.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260304184224.htm

3. **Key protein found essential for malaria parasites** : 05 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Researchers identified Aurora-related kinase 1 (ARK1) as vital for malaria parasite cell division, with its deactivation halting replication in humans and mosquitoes. This discovery points to targeted treatments that could interrupt the parasite's life cycle and reduce disease spread.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260304184221.htm

4. **Fastest photodetector senses full light spectrum** : 05 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Duke University engineers created an ultrathin photodetector that detects light across the electromagnetic spectrum in 125 picoseconds. It enables advanced multispectral cameras for applications in medicine, agriculture, and space exploration.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260304184218.htm

5. **Drug slashes seizures in rare childhood epilepsy** : 05 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Zorevunersen reduced seizures by up to 91% in Dravet syndrome trials by enhancing a key gene for nerve signaling. A Phase 3 trial is underway, offering hope for better management of this severe genetic condition.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260304184215.htm

6. **GLP-1 drugs aid heart recovery post-attack** : 05 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Medications like Ozempic and Wegovy improved blood flow in small vessels after heart attacks in new research. Beyond weight loss, they may protect cardiac health by restoring microvascular function.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260304184223.htm

7. **Reassessing genome editing regulations for plants** : 05 March 2026 • Nature  
   Experts argue that classifying site-directed nucleases overlooks modern genome editing diversity, advocating for outcome-based regulations in plant breeding. This shift could accelerate climate-resilient crop development amid evolving environmental challenges.  
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-026-03028-0

8. **Synchrotron creates vast 3D ant image library** : 05 March 2026 • Nature  
   High-throughput X-ray microtomography produced thousands of 3D images capturing ant diversity. This scalable resource advances invertebrate digitization for ecological and evolutionary studies.  
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03004-1

9. **TREM2 antibody tested in early Alzheimer’s trial** : 05 March 2026 • Nature  
   The AL002 antibody engaged targets but failed to improve Clinical Dementia Rating scores in a phase 2 trial for early Alzheimer’s. Further analysis may refine approaches to modulating brain immune responses.  
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04273-1

10. **Obesity drugs show promise against addiction** : 05 March 2026 • Nature  
   A study of 600,000 veterans found anti-obesity medications reduced substance-use disorder complications. This suggests broader therapeutic potential for managing addiction-related risks.  
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00734-2

11. **Daily aspirin’s weak role in preventing colon cancer** : 04 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   A review found daily aspirin offers unreliable bowel cancer prevention for average-risk people, with benefits delayed and bleeding risks immediate. Decisions should weigh individual factors over routine use.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260303145733.htm

12. **Exercise emerges as top osteoarthritis treatment** : 04 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Research emphasizes exercise over surgery or drugs for osteoarthritis, as it nourishes cartilage, strengthens muscles, and curbs inflammation. This could transform management for the 600 million affected worldwide.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260303145725.htm

13. **Fruits and veggies linked to higher pesticide exposure** : 04 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Eating produce like strawberries and spinach with known high pesticide residues increased urinary chemical levels in participants. Findings underscore how diet influences exposure to substances tied to health risks.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260303145705.htm

14. **Boreal wildfires release overlooked ancient carbon** : 04 March 2026 • Science Daily  
   Fires in northern forests burn deep into peat, emitting ancient carbon underestimated by satellite models. This reveals greater climate impacts from smoldering, hard-to-detect blazes.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260303201755.htm

15. **Resistance training slows elderly brain aging** : 04 March 2026 • Lifespan.io  
   MRI-based brain clock models showed one year of resistance exercise reduced apparent brain age in older adults. It offers a practical way to combat cognitive decline through physical activity.  
   Source: https://lifespan.io/news/resistance-exercise-training-slows-down-brain-aging/?utm_source=rss&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=resistance-exercise-training-slows-down-brain-aging

### Planetterrian Spotlight
The discovery that resistance training can slow brain aging stands out for its direct tie to longevity. By analyzing MRI images, researchers found that a year of such exercises effectively "rewound" brain age markers in elderly participants, suggesting it bolsters neural health beyond just physical fitness. This could empower more people to incorporate weight-based routines into daily life, potentially delaying age-related cognitive issues. What to watch: larger trials exploring how it combines with diet or other interventions. How might you weave resistance training into your routine for long-term brain benefits?
