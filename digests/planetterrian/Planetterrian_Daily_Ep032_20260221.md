2. **Pneumonia Bacterium Linked to Alzheimer’s: 21 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   Chlamydia pneumoniae, known for pneumonia, may contribute to Alzheimer’s by causing inflammation and amyloid-beta buildup in the brain. This discovery could lead to new preventive strategies for cognitive decline, especially for high-risk individuals.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260221000321.htm

3. **Fragile X Brain Wave Biomarker Found: 20 February 2026 • MIT News**
   Researchers identified matching low-frequency brain wave differences in humans and mice with fragile X syndrome. This biomarker paves the way for targeted autism treatments and better research models.
   Source: https://news.mit.edu/2026/fragile-x-study-uncovers-brainwave-biomarker-bridging-humans-mice-0220

4. **AI Tool Excels in Rare Disease Diagnosis: 20 February 2026 • Lifespan.io**

5. **Semaglutide May Ease Arthritis Pain: 20 February 2026 • Science News**
   A study shows semaglutide could strengthen cartilage in osteoarthritis patients, based on trials in mice and humans. This offers hope for reducing joint pain and improving mobility for millions.
   Source: https://www.sciencenews.org/article/ozempic-osteoarthritis-arthritis-semaglutide

6. **Exercise Shields Brain from Alzheimer’s: 20 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   Physical activity triggers a liver enzyme that repairs the blood-brain barrier, reducing inflammation and boosting memory in older mice. This body-to-brain link could inspire novel Alzheimer’s therapies to protect cognitive health.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260219040752.htm

7. **Human Language Efficiency Over Digital Code: 20 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   Research shows human language, unlike computer code, uses predictable patterns for easier brain processing despite seeming inefficient. This understanding could enhance communication aids for neurological conditions.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260219040811.htm

8. **Quantum Computer Tracks Qubit Fluctuations: 20 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   Scientists at NBI developed a system to monitor qubit changes in real time, 100 times faster than before. This breakthrough could stabilize quantum processors, advancing computing for medical and scientific solutions.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260219040756.htm

9. **Mapping Deep Earth Mantle Earthquakes: 20 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   Stanford researchers created the first global map of rare mantle earthquakes, identifying clusters in regions like the Himalayas. Understanding these tremors could improve geological models and disaster preparedness.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260219040818.htm
