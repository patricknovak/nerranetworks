### Top 15 Science & Health Discoveries
1. **Microbe Genome Pushes Life’s Limits: 19 February 2026 • New Scientist**
   A symbiotic microbe inside insect cells has the smallest genome ever recorded, evolved over millions of years. This discovery challenges our understanding of life’s minimal requirements and could inspire bioengineering for sustainable microbial solutions.
   Source: https://www.newscientist.com/article/2516163-microbe-with-the-smallest-genome-yet-pushes-the-boundaries-of-life/?utm_campaign=RSS%7CNSNS&utm_source=NSNS&utm_medium=RSS&utm_content=home

2. **AI Replaces Rare Earth Magnets: 19 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   University of New Hampshire scientists used AI to identify 67,573 magnetic compounds, including 25 high-temperature magnets for electric vehicles. This reduces reliance on scarce materials, advancing sustainable transport and cutting environmental mining impacts.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260218031611.htm

3. **Sodium Battery Doubles Energy, Desalinates: 19 February 2026 • Science Daily**
   A new sodium-ion battery stores twice the energy and desalinates seawater by retaining water in its material. This dual-purpose innovation promises cheaper energy storage and access to clean water, supporting resource-scarce regions sustainably.
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260218031603.htm

4. **Microalgae as Crystal Factories: 19 February 2026 • Nature**
   Dinoflagellates in microalgae crystallize nitrogen-rich molecules, revealing potential for marine nitrogen cycling insights. This could lead to bioengineered materials with optical uses while promoting eco-friendly production methods.
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-026-03017-3

5. **Microalgae Biosynthesize Optical Crystals: 19 February 2026 • Nature**
   Living dinoflagellates are used to synthesize crystalline materials for optical applications with tailored properties. Such biological manufacturing opens doors to sustainable tech innovations with minimal environmental footprint.
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-026-03006-6

6. **Trimodal Cell Profiling Unlocks Regulation: 19 February 2026 • Nature**
   A new trimodal single-cell analysis maps transcriptome, epigenome, and 3D genome in complex tissues. This deepens our grasp of gene regulation, paving the way for precise medical therapies.
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-026-03013-7

7. **DeepMind’s AlphaFold 4 Amazes Scientists: 19 February 2026 • Nature**
   Isomorphic Labs’ proprietary AI model for drug discovery marks a leap beyond open-source tools. It promises faster, targeted treatments, potentially transforming health outcomes globally.
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00365-7

8. **Plasma Test Predicts Alzheimer’s Onset: 19 February 2026 • Nature**
   Plasma p-tau217 tests create clocks to predict when cognitively unimpaired individuals may show Alzheimer’s symptoms. Early detection could revolutionize care, enabling timely interventions for better quality of life.
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04206-y

9. **T Cell Engagers Treat Autoimmune Diseases: 19 February 2026 • Nature**
   Bispecific T cell engagers like blinatumomab improved outcomes in treatment-refractory autoimmune diseases in a small patient series. This offers hope for managing severe conditions with fewer side effects.
   Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04238-4

10. **Paediatrician Blood Fights RSV, Colds: 18 February 2026 • New Scientist**
    Antibodies from paediatricians’ blood are 25 times more effective against RSV, now being developed as preventative treatments. This could protect vulnerable children from common respiratory infections.
    Source: https://www.newscientist.com/article/2516079-paediatricians-blood-used-to-make-new-treatments-for-rsv-and-colds/?utm_campaign=RSS%7CNSNS&utm_source=NSNS&utm_medium=RSS&utm_content=home

11. **HPV Vaccine Boosts Cancer Defense: 18 February 2026 • Science Daily**
    A reengineered HPV vaccine repositions a protein fragment to enhance T cell attacks on tumors in animal models. This design slows cancer growth, offering a potent tool against HPV-driven cancers.
    Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260216044006.htm

12. **Gyroscope Unlocks Wave Energy: 18 February 2026 • Science Daily**
    A gyroscopic wave energy converter at Osaka University uses a spinning flywheel to turn ocean waves into electricity. This could tap a vast renewable source, reducing fossil fuel dependency.
    Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260218031554.htm

13. **Moon Shrinking Triggers Quakes: 18 February 2026 • Science Daily**
    Over a thousand new tectonic ridges show the Moon is still shrinking, potentially causing more moonquakes. Understanding these seismic risks is vital for future lunar missions and human safety.
    Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260218031532.htm

14. **Cognitive Enrichment Cuts Dementia Risk: 18 February 2026 • Lifespan.io**
    Lifelong cognitive enrichment is linked to reduced dementia risk, delaying onset by five to seven years. This highlights the power of mental engagement for healthier aging.
    Source: https://lifespan.io/news/lifetime-cognitive-enrichment-associated-with-less-dementia/?utm_source=rss&utm_medium=rss&utm_campaign=lifetime-cognitive-enrichment-associated-with-less-dementia

15. **Glass Stores Data for Millennia: 18 February 2026 • New Scientist**
    Microsoft’s laser technology writes data into glass, envisioning robotic libraries of durable tablets. This could preserve critical health and scientific knowledge with minimal energy use over centuries.
    Source: https://www.newscientist.com/article/2516075-data-centres-could-store-information-in-glass-for-thousands-of-years/?utm_campaign=RSS%7CNSNS&utm_source=NSNS&utm_medium=RSS&utm_content=home
