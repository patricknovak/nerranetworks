### Top 10 Science & Health Discoveries
1. **Scientists find a hidden obesity trigger in soybean oil: 30 November, 2025, 07:46 AM PST, Science Daily**  
   Researchers at UC Riverside have discovered that soybean oil, a common ingredient in American diets, promotes significant weight gain in mice due to mechanisms beyond the oil's calories. This breakthrough highlights the importance of understanding food ingredients for combating obesity, which could lead to healthier, sustainable dietary choices that support human longevity and reduce the environmental impact of industrial agriculture. By promoting plant-based alternatives with fewer hidden risks, we can foster compassionate innovations that benefit both personal well-being and planetary health.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251129044503.htm

2. **Your skin has a built-in cancer defense and sunlight turns it off: 30 November, 2025, 06:33 AM PST, Science Daily**  
   Scientists have revealed how excessive sunlight deactivates a natural inflammation-control mechanism in skin cells, heightening cancer risks. This finding empowers us to develop sustainable sun protection strategies and therapies that enhance the body's innate defenses, promoting longer, healthier lives while encouraging eco-friendly habits like mindful outdoor time to preserve our planet's biodiversity. It's a compassionate reminder that harmonizing human health with nature's rhythms can drive innovative, earth-conscious solutions.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251129044505.htm

3. **Scientists discover a hidden brain circuit that rewrites vision: 30 November, 2025, 03:42 AM PST, Science Daily**  
   MIT researchers identified brain signals from planning and control areas that modulate visual perception based on alertness levels. Understanding this could revolutionize treatments for vision-related disorders, enhancing cognitive longevity and enabling sustainable technologies like bio-inspired AI that minimize energy use in devices, ultimately benefiting human well-being and environmental conservation. This discovery inspires forward-thinking approaches to brain health that align with our planet's delicate ecosystems.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251130050715.htm

4. **Why some memories last a lifetime while others fade fast: 30 November, 2025, 03:13 AM PST, Science Daily**  
   A new study outlines a stepwise brain process for forming and stabilizing long-term memories through virtual reality experiments. This insight could advance therapies for memory loss in aging populations, supporting extended healthy lifespans and sustainable mental health practices that reduce reliance on resource-intensive pharmaceuticals, fostering a compassionate future where innovation nurtures both minds and the earth.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251130050712.htm

5. **242-million-year-old mini predator changes lizard evolution: 30 November, 2025, 01:09 AM PST, Science Daily**  
   A fossil from Devon reveals unexpected traits in early lizard ancestors, challenging evolutionary timelines. This paleontological find deepens our appreciation for biodiversity's history, inspiring conservation efforts that protect modern reptiles and promote sustainable ecosystems, linking ancient discoveries to compassionate actions for planetary longevity and human enlightenment.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251129044520.htm

6. **Dinosaur mummy found with hooves and a hidden crest: 30 November, 2025, 12:47 AM PST, Science Daily**  
   An exceptionally preserved Edmontosaurus fossil, reconstructed via clay templating, unveils detailed features like hooves and a crest. This vivid glimpse into prehistoric life encourages sustainable paleontology practices and educates on evolutionary adaptations, bridging ancient wonders with modern efforts to safeguard biodiversity for healthier, longer-lived species—including our own—on a thriving planet.  
   Source: https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251129044518.htm
